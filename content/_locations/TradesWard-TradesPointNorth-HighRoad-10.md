# Archibald's Folios (and the Caedlin Building)

## Overview
_Books & Broadsheets._ The half-orc bookseller Archibald Strongjaw has an impressive array of reading intended for casual perusal rather than a sage's work-shelf. He sells a great deal of adventure pulps – of both bawdy and tame content – and particularly specializes in vaeterann content, making his shop a defacto site of Twilight Vaeteru for Waterdeep. He is also the go-to place for locals looking for any of the many broadsheets printed on a daily basis in Waterdeep. Archibald owns the building, but doesn't need a lot of space for his shop; he does, however, keep the cellars of the building to himself, and the stairs that descend just outside his shop door are considered private (although tenants know they are welcome to drop by his shop at any time).

### First Floor
**Sunbright Family Jewelers:** _Jewelry_. Next to Archibald's, between that shop and Nanny's next door, is the small jewelry shop of Qulestha Sunbright, a half-elven jeweler and lapidary. She maintains a small showroom out front and a backroom with a workbench, a pair of small safes, and a desk from which she runs her business. Given the value of the goods she keeps on hand, one of her apprentices bunks down in this workroom at night, to keep thieves away.
**Rooms:** The back of the building has four single dwelling rooms, which Archibald rents out. Two of them are rented by Qulestha, using one as living space for her and her husband Milinar (a driver of one of the ubiquitous Waterdhavian dray wagons), and the other as space for their almost-adult son Rundeth and Qulestha's apprentices Linthos and Urenda (there are only two beds, since the apprentices take turns sleeping in the workroom of the shop at night). The third room is rented to Ylidar, a journeyman baker who works in a bakery a few blocks away, who frequently brings home end-of-day breads for his neighbors. The fourth room is unoccupied, though Archibald is seeking a tenant.
### Cellars
**Archibald's Cellar:** The bottom of the stairs from the street level above leads to a small foyer. To the left is Archibald's office and repository, where he stores most of the books that he does not keep on the shelves above (or rather, which he tends to cycle out in order to provide a continually-fresh custom). To the right is his living quarters: brick-floored but nonetheless comfortable bathing chamber and dining room along the north wall, and his sitting and bedrooms along the south wall with a very modest kitchen at the far end of the main hall there.
### Second Floor
**Golden Imports:** _Imports._ Recently, the young handsome Surinan replaced his old uncle Miqab as proprietor of this import shop, after Waterdeep's winters became too much for the old Calishite man's bones. Surinan is finding his new freedom in the City of Splendors to be quite the delight, although his sampling of its wonders has led to a few mornings of late openings. Golden Imports sells Calishite goods of all sorts, taking handcrafts considered common in the markets of Calimport and charging significantly more for them in Waterdeep. Surinan's other responsibilities are to do the same in reverse, finding low-price local goods and shipping them to his family's "genuine Northern treasures" shop in Calimport.
**Kavrenan's Hues:** _Artists Supplies._ Kavrenan's Hues is an excellent stop-off for folk who value good quality paints and artists supplies. Such custom comes rarely, though, so Kavrenan usually sits out on the front tradewalk, attempting his latest Waterdhavian cityscape. Sadly, he's a much better purveyor of art goods than he is an artist, but he clearly enjoys himself while he works. It's not uncommon to find Surinan out in front with him to share a tea or hookah between them.
**Rooms to Let:** The six small rooms behind the shopfronts on this level are modest to say the least. Kavrenan keeps a room for himself, and Surinan rents the three behind his shop, one as extra storage space and two as very basic bechambers  (not for himself, but for when his family's business has visiting buyers or other agents). A pair of newlyweds – a tiefling named Abra and her human husband Firth – rent one of the other rooms. Abra does cleaning work, including for many of the shops in this building, and Firth ventures out for physical labor, though he is attempting to secure an apprenticeship with the Fellowship of Carters and Coachmen.
### Third Floor
**Office of Desna Ilitun:** _Clerk & Bookkeep._ Desna is a no-nonsense human journey-woman in the Scriveners', Scribes, and Clerks Guild. She operates her business of aiding business-owners with the paperwork, book-keeping, legal documents, and correspondence necessary to function. She prefers for clients to come to her, bringing her the materials she needs to do work for them, and quite a few regularly deliver receipts, letters, and legal papers to her for exactly that service. She also is willing to make appointments to visit a business and work for them from there, for an additional fee.
**Silken Stories:** _Embroidery._ Dressed in beautifully embroidered robes, the half-elf Miliranwë is an embroiderer. His storefront displays examples of his work, from fine garment details to impressive tapestry-work. His workspace blazes with light, given the delicate detail-work he does, and he lives in the small backroom of the shop.
**Rooms to Let:** Desna maintains a room here, and she and Miliranwë have just begun a romance among neighbors, so they often spend their time together in her space. Two of the rooms here are unoccupied, and the other two are lived in by a family of four, the Oveleskur family. Davros Oveleskur is a a lantern-guide by night, and his wife Emelenn works at the Dusken Glade in the kitchens during the day. Their two children Umala and Genvros stay with whichever parent is there, although ten year old Umala can occasionally be found running messages and parcels between local shops, including fetching foodstuffs for shop-keeps who can't afford to leave their business for a meal.
